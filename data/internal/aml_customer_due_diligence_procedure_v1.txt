AML CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE PROCEDURE v1.0

PURPOSE
To establish comprehensive Know Your Customer (KYC) and Customer Due Diligence (CDD) processes compliant with Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002 and Schedule-A requirements.

SCOPE
All customer onboarding, relationship enhancement, and periodic review activities.

KYC REQUIREMENTS

1. CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION PROGRAM (CIP)
1.1 For Individual Customers:
    - Full legal name (as per government-issued ID)
    - Date of birth
    - Permanent address and contact details
    - Occupation and source of income
    - PAN (Permanent Account Number) or equivalent
    - Identity proof (Aadhaar/Passport/Driving License/Voter ID)
    - Address proof (not older than 3 months)

1.2 For Business Entities:
    - Legal entity name and registration number
    - Business address and operational address
    - Principal business activity and industry classification
    - Beneficial ownership details (>25% stake holders)
    - Certificate of Incorporation or Registration
    - Board resolution authorizing relationship
    - Directors' identification documents

1.3 For High-Risk Customers:
    - Enhanced identification verification required
    - Source of wealth/income documented
    - Enhanced address verification (on-site if necessary)
    - Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening completed

2. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
2.1 Risk Categorization:
    - Low-risk: Standard documentation sufficient
    - Medium-risk: Additional verification and monitoring required
    - High-risk: Enhanced due diligence mandatory

2.2 Risk Assessment Factors:
    - Customer profile and background
    - Geographic location and jurisdiction
    - Nature and frequency of transactions
    - Exposure to jurisdictions with weak AML controls
    - Customer's compliance history

3. DOCUMENTATION AND VERIFICATION
3.1 Document Collection:
    - Collect and verify all identity documents
    - Maintain certified copies in customer file
    - Digital copies stored with encrypted access

3.2 Verification Process:
    - Identity verified from original documents
    - Address verification conducted through independent means
    - Bank account verification completed before funds receipt
    - PEP screening performed against regulatory lists

4. ONGOING MONITORING AND REVIEW
4.1 Customer Information Update:
    - Annual review of customer information
    - Additional information requested if profile changes
    - Address reverification every 3 years minimum
    - Transaction pattern monitoring for suspicious activity

4.2 Periodic CDD Review:
    - File review conducted annually
    - Risk categorization reassessed
    - Enhanced monitoring upgraded if risk indicators emerge

5. RECORD MAINTENANCE
5.1 All KYC and CDD records maintained:
    - Minimum retention period: 5 years post-relationship closure
    - Secure storage with restricted access
    - Digital backup maintained in encrypted format

6. SCREENING AND SANCTION CHECKS
6.1 Sanctions Screening:
    - Customer screening against UN, UNSC, and national sanction lists
    - Beneficial owners screened against regulatory lists
    - Transaction counterparties screened before payment
    - Quarterly rescreening of existing customers

ESCALATION AND REPORTING
If customer fails verification or presents high risk:
- Escalated to Compliance Officer
- Relationship declined or suspended
- SAR filed if suspicious activity detected
- Management review completed

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
This procedure complies with Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002, Schedule-A obligations, and guidance issued by Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU).
