CRIMINAL CASE INTAKE PROCEDURE v1.0

PURPOSE
This procedure ensures consistent and documented intake of criminal cases to maintain chain of custody and legal compliance with Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 requirements.

SCOPE
Applicable to all case intake personnel, legal advisors, and evidence custodians.

PROCEDURE

1. INITIAL INTAKE
1.1 All incoming criminal complaints shall be registered with unique case ID within 24 hours of receipt.
1.2 Complainant identity verified through valid government-issued identification.
1.3 Preliminary assessment conducted to determine jurisdiction and urgency level.

2. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
2.1 Case intake form completed including:
    - Complainant details and contact information
    - Alleged offense category and description
    - Incident date, time, and location
    - Preliminary evidence list
    - Assigned investigator name and contact

2.2 All forms must be signed by intake officer and complainant within intake session.
2.3 Digital copies maintained in case management system with encrypted access.

3. EVIDENCE HANDLING
3.1 Physical evidence logged with description, condition, and custody record.
3.2 Chain of custody form initiated and maintained throughout case lifecycle.
3.3 Evidence stored in designated secure facility with access log.

4. CASE ASSIGNMENT
4.1 Cases assigned to qualified investigators within 48 hours based on specialization.
4.2 Assignment documented with investigator acknowledgment.

5. NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
5.1 Complainant notified of case registration within 72 hours.
5.2 Statutory notifications issued per Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita requirements.
5.3 All notifications documented with delivery confirmation.

COMPLIANCE NOTES
This procedure aligns with Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 sections on criminal procedure and evidence handling requirements.
