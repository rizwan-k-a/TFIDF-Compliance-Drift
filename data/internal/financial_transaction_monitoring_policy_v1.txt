FINANCIAL TRANSACTION MONITORING POLICY v1.0

PURPOSE
To establish systematic monitoring and controls over financial transactions to prevent money laundering, fraud, and unauthorized fund transfers in compliance with Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002.

SCOPE
All financial transactions conducted by the organization, including payments, receipts, transfers, and settlements.

MONITORING FRAMEWORK

1. TRANSACTION CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM
1.1 All transactions categorized by:
    - Amount threshold (micro/small/medium/large)
    - Transaction type (domestic/international)
    - Counterparty category (known/unknown/high-risk)
    - Business purpose classification

1.2 Risk scoring algorithm applied:
    - Transactions exceeding defined thresholds flagged automatically
    - Unusual patterns detected through statistical analysis
    - High-risk jurisdiction transactions require enhanced scrutiny

2. THRESHOLD MONITORING
2.1 Transactions exceeding INR 10 lakhs subject to enhanced verification:
    - Purpose documentation required
    - Counterparty identity verification
    - Supporting invoice/contract review
    - Approval from designated authority

2.2 Cumulative transaction monitoring:
    - Daily aggregate limits monitored
    - Monthly transaction volume reviewed
    - Unusual spike patterns escalated

3. SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING
3.1 Suspicious transactions identified if:
    - Inconsistent with customer profile
    - Structuring patterns observed
    - High-risk jurisdictions involved
    - Weak Know Your Customer (KYC) documentation

3.2 Internal reporting requirements:
    - Flagged transaction documented within 24 hours
    - Escalated to Compliance Officer for assessment
    - Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) prepared if warranted
    - Filed with Financial Intelligence Unit if required

4. DOCUMENTATION AND AUDIT TRAIL
4.1 All monitoring activities logged:
    - Transaction ID, amount, counterparty
    - Risk assessment score
    - Approval authority
    - Monitoring conclusion

4.2 Records maintained for minimum 5 years:
    - Secure encrypted storage required
    - Access restricted to authorized personnel only
    - Audit trail maintained for all record access

5. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE
5.1 High-risk transactions require:
    - Source of funds verification
    - Beneficial ownership documentation
    - Business purpose detailed explanation
    - Director/management approval

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
This policy ensures compliance with Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 and related guidelines issued by financial regulators.
